Let X represent a binary coin flip. Then
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(X = 1) = p,&& P(X = 0) = 1 - p
\end{aligned}
$$
for some $p \in [0, 1]$. We say that $X$ has a
Bernoulli distribution written $X \sim \operatorname{Bernoulli}(p)$. The probability function is
$$
f(x) = p^x(1 - p)^{1 - x}
$$
for $x \in \{0, 1\}$.
